FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What’s the process of installing an EV Charger?

If you, the customer, are searching for a rough estimate of cost, we might be able to do it over the phone. However, before we proceed with the charging system installation, we must visit your home to provide you a firm quote - which is free! We then check the location of the charger installation, which is typically in your garage. We need to know whether your garage is attached or detached so we can place the conduit or cable accordingly.After that, we discuss what kind of charger you want — Stage 1, 2 or 3. Most homeowners are looking for Stage 2. Finally, you will need to decide whether you want just the 14-50R receptacle installed to give you the option to plug in your corded stage 2 charger yourself or you want the actual EV charger installed as well, whether it’s hardwired or corded.

What is the difference between Level 1, Level 2 and Level 3 charging?

Stage 1 is a small 15-amp charger (usually included with the purchase of your electric vehicle), Stage 2 is a 40-amp charger that requires a 50-amp circuit, and Stage 3, also commonly known as DC fast charging, charges through a 480V DC plug, which is more to benefit businesses and commercial areas. A higher amperage allows for more current to flow faster to your electric car. This will reduce charging times significantly.

How long do EV chargers last?

Like all electrical equipment, they don’t last forever. Currently, their lifespan is projected to be 20-30 years.

Do you only install EV charging stations?

No - we provide full electrical services, from solar panel installations, smart home, complete led solutions, addition of new circuits, installation of smoke detectors, electrical repairs and upgrade, new construction wiring and much more!
